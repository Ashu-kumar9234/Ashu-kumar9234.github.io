Use of Medicine

Treat diseases Medicines can cure infections, treat cancer, and help control chronic conditions like diabetes, asthma, and high blood pressure Improve health Medicines can help you get healthy when you're sick, and can help you feel better while you're getting well Prevent illness Medicines can help prevent some of the leading causes of death and disability. Immunizations, like the flu shot, can help prevent serious illnesses Relieve pain Pain relievers can help you feel better while you're getting well Replace missing substances Medicines can replace missing substances or correct low levels of natural body chemicals like hormones or vitamins Safety and risks All medicines, both prescription and over-the-counter, have risks as well as benefits. You should think through the benefits and the risks before using any medicine. Regulatory agencies In the United States, the Food and Drug Administration (FDA) is responsible for ensuring that medicines are safe and effective.
